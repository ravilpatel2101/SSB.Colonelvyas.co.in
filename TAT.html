TAT

Thematic Apperception Test

What is TAT?

Thematic apperception test is a projective psychological test on day 2. Proponents of this technique assert that a person’s responses reveal underlying motives, concerns, and the way they see the social world through the stories they make up about ambiguous pictures of people. Basically this test helps in understanding weather a person is of the mentallity what arm forces want to its officers.


What to do in TAT?

In TAT a candidate will be asked to write a story from his own based on the images shown. Consider writing a completely fresh story with completely unique names (And NOT Ram, Shyam, Gopal...).


Important points to note

  • 1. Each story must have a chief character (Hero), it will be wise to use officer like qualities (OLQs) when creating the personality traits of the character.
  • 2. Let the hero and character have some decent name and not Ram, Shyam and Gopal. Let the names of your dear friends come in it.
  • 3. Choose the age and profession of the main lead of your story very carefully. The age should be exactly what is evident from the picture. And choose the profession according to the age.
  • 4. Write Practical story and not superficial based stories.
  • 5. Always try giving a sucessful and happy ending. The story should have a positive sense and not negative.
  • 6. Keep a keen eye when looking at picture, note each and every small point.
